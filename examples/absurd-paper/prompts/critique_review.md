## Self-critique requirements

Polish the draft with these passes — keep what works, surgically fix what
doesn't:

- **Voice consistency.** Every paragraph must sound like the *same*
  reviewer talking. Excise lines that drift into a generic-academic
  register.
- **Recommendation conviction.** Re-read your own recommendation
  paragraph: does it commit, or does it hedge? If it hedges, sharpen
  the verdict.
- **Concrete critique.** Replace any vague "the methodology is unclear"
  language with a specific gripe (a missing baseline, an unstated
  assumption, an unjustified threshold). Keep at least three concrete
  technical critiques.
- **Conclusion verdict.** Your draft should already evaluate the paper's
  Conclusion explicitly — verify it does, and tighten that paragraph if
  the verdict is buried.
- **Citation mix audit.** Confirm the draft notes whether the paper grounds
  itself in real foundational references vs. only fabricated ones; add
  this as a strength or weakness if the draft missed it.
- **Persona artifacts.** Add one in-character flourish that only
  *this* reviewer would write — a digression, a backhanded compliment,
  a methodological obsession (whatever fits **{{style}}**).
- **Length.** Keep within 350-550 words. Cut filler before adding
  content.
